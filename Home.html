Phone Scam is a Growing Threat

According to figures released by the Australian Bureau of Statistics (ABS), 65% of Australians received a scam offer or request in 2021-22, up from 55% in the previous year. People were most commonly exposed to a scam over the phone which has increased from 38% in 2020-21 to 48% in 2021-22.

We change the numbers

Giving scammers the "perfect victim"

Scammers love to talk. They spin up stories and craft webs of lies to get you on the hook. What if there was a way to let scammers talk and talk and talk but never make a dime.

The "perfect victim" isn't a victim at all

We are creating a smart, AI-driven bot to do the listening and the talking, wasting scammers time while offering no rewards.
More time wasted = less time scamming.

What are we offering?

Society

  • If scammers spend their days talking to robots, their business model will go bust
  • As a result, we can keep money in the pockets of the honest.

Companies

  • Companies can also stay up to date on the newest scams and keep everyone in the know.

People
